A Cat's Instinct

Cats are practically born to hunt. Many things we see them do like stalking, pouncing, and clawing were all tethered bach to their main instinct: to hunt. Their other instincts include: keeping themselves clean, having their own territory, and staying up on high places.
 
 

Differences Through Time

Many studies have shown the differences in cat's brains and bodies through domestication. Science has shown that cats now respond, can recognize emotion, and are responsive to being called.

Voicing Their Words

Cats are excellent communicators they vocalize more than 100 sounds! Cats can meow, purr, hiss, spit, and grunt to exchange information with humans and other cats alike. Cats actually have a more similar brain to us than they do to dogs.
